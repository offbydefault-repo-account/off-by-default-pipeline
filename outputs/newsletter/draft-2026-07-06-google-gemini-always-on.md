## DRAFT BEGINS

There used to be a small button. A sparkle icon, tucked into the corner of Gmail. You could click it if you wanted AI help drafting something. You could ignore it if you didn't. That was the deal — optional, visible, yours to decide.

At Google I/O in May 2026, Google announced that deal is over. Gemini — Google's AI — is no longer a button you press. It's the layer underneath everything, active whether you pressed anything or not. By July, that rollout is underway.

If you use Gmail, Google Docs, Google Drive, or Google Search, this affects your account right now.

---

Google's announcement framed it differently: Gemini is now "woven through" all of Google's products as an always-on presence. That language is doing a lot of work.

Here's what most coverage of this story skips: the Gemini-in-Search change is not just a new feature. It's a change to what Google Search is.

For more than twenty years, Google Search worked the same way: you typed something, Google showed you a ranked list of links to other people's pages, and you decided which one to click. That model put the original author at the other end of your click — they got the traffic, and the attention, and theoretically the compensation that came from it.

The new model works differently: you type something, Google shows you an AI-generated answer assembled from other people's pages, and the links appear below it. Many users never scroll down. The people who wrote the pages being summarized — journalists, bloggers, creators, researchers — are still not compensated for their content being used this way. Traffic to original sources is measurably declining. [SOURCE NEEDED: cite a specific recent stat on publisher traffic decline from AI search overviews] This is not a side effect. It is the product.
