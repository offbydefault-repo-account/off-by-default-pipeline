### SETUP — The problem stated plainly

Meta AI is woven into Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp. You may have used it without making a big decision about it — asking for restaurant recommendations, getting help writing a caption, searching for something you needed.

Those conversations feel casual. They read like a chat. Starting December 2025, they became something else: advertising signals.

Meta updated its privacy policy. No pop-up, no announcement. Users in most of the world — including the US — didn't get a notice and didn't get a choice.

---

### EXPLANATION — What's actually happening

Here's how it works in practice.

You ask Meta AI: "What hiking boots are good for beginners?" That topic gets logged. You're now categorized as someone interested in hiking gear. Ads for boots, trail apps, and outdoor gear start appearing in your feed.

Meta says it does not use "sensitive" topics — religion, health, politics, sexual orientation — for this targeting. [VERIFY THIS before scripting the final version — check if this claim has been independently tested or is only Meta's stated policy]

Here's what you can't check: there's no log, no dashboard, no audit trail that shows you what your conversations were classified as or what advertising signals they generated. You're taking Meta's word for it.

The chatbot framing does real psychological work here. Most people understand they're generating data when they're shopping online. A conversation feels different — even if the data outcome is similar. That gap is not an accident.

### NUANCE — The thing most explainer videos skip

Almost every video on this will say: "just opt out."

Here's the problem. If you're in the US, you can't. Not fully.

Users in the EU, the UK, and South Korea have meaningful opt-outs, because regulators in those places required Meta to build them. American users have a setting — buried in Privacy > AI Data Usage — but it's limited. [VERIFY THIS — confirm exactly what the US setting covers]

That difference is not a technical limitation. The opt-out already exists. Meta built it for Europe. The decision was made not to give it to Americans.

And here's the pattern worth seeing: Meta isn't uniquely bad here. Google's AI is inside Gmail and Docs. Microsoft's Copilot is inside Word and Outlook. Amazon's Alexa is in homes. All of them have policies describing how your AI interactions get used. Meta's is just unusually direct about the advertising connection — and unusual in having no US opt-out.

### TAKEAWAY — One thing to remember or do

Here's the one thing to take from this:

When a product you didn't pay for learns from private conversations, the product was never the chatbot.

**What to do right now:**
Go into your Meta account settings. Find AI Data Usage or similar under privacy. Read what it covers — not just what it says, but what it doesn't say.

For anything personal — health questions, financial questions, anything you'd lower your voice to say — stop using Meta AI. Treat it the same way you'd treat speaking near an open microphone you don't control. That's not paranoia. That's just understanding what it is.
