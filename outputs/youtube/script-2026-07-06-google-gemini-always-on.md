### HOOK (first 15 seconds)

If you've used Google Search in the last few weeks and felt like something was different — like you're getting answers instead of links — you're not wrong. Google made a major change to how all of its products work, and most people haven't heard about it.

Here's what changed, what it means for your data, and what you can actually do.

---

### SETUP — What Google announced

At Google I/O in May 2026, Google announced that Gemini — its AI — was done being a chatbot you open in a separate tab.

Instead, Gemini is now an "always-on layer" woven through Gmail, Google Docs, Google Drive, Google Sheets, and Google Search. It's not a feature you turn on. It's the new baseline.

By July, that rollout is underway. If you use any of these products — and most people do — this has already changed for you.

---

### EXPLANATION — What "always-on" actually looks like

In Google Search, the change is already visible. The AI-generated answer appears before the links. Not as an experiment. Not as a separate mode. As the default. Here's the part that matters: for any of this to work, Gemini has to read your content. Your emails. Your documents. Your files. [VERIFY THIS: confirm this policy covers both free and paid accounts, and note any exceptions] The AI reads your content in real time to generate responses, but doesn't keep it in the sense of building future models from it.

That distinction matters. But it's still access. And it's access that is now on by default.

### NUANCE — The thing most explainer videos skip

Here's the part of this story that almost nobody is covering, and it's bigger than the Gmail feature.

When Google puts Gemini in Search — when the AI answer comes first, before the links — it changes what Google Search fundamentally is.

For twenty-plus years, Google Search was a directory. You asked a question. Google pointed you to the person who answered it. You clicked. They got the visit. The writer, the reporter, the blogger, the creator — they got the traffic.

The new model: you ask a question, Gemini answers it from dozens of pages, and you see the links below. Many people never scroll down. The people who wrote those pages are still not compensated for their content being used this way. Traffic to original sources is measurably dropping. "Gemini is in Search" sounds like Google added a feature. What it actually did is change who benefits from the web — and in which direction.
