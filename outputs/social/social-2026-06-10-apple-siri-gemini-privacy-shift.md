## LINKEDIN

Apple has spent over a decade making a very specific promise. Not just "we care about privacy." The specific promise: your data stays with you. They put it on billboards. Built ad campaigns around it. The comedian in the elevator, panicking about being overheard.

Yesterday at WWDC, they announced Siri will be powered by Google Gemini for queries Apple's own AI can't handle.

Apple's existing system already moved some data off your device — but to Apple's own Private Cloud Compute, with audited commitments: no employee access, no data retention after requests, no training on your queries. Not on-device, but a meaningfully higher standard than most cloud AI services.

Gemini is a different category. When a query goes to Google, it's under Google's infrastructure and Google's privacy policy.

The piece that hasn't been documented yet is what happens to "personal context." Apple's new opt-in feature lets Siri read your calendar, messages, and email to give more relevant answers. Does that context travel with the query when it gets routed to Gemini? Nobody has published an answer to that yet.

These features don't ship until iOS 27 in the fall. That window is useful. The technical documentation will come — from Apple, and from independent privacy researchers who will analyze it. That analysis is worth reading before you opt in to anything.

More at the link — including what to pay attention to and what can wait. [NEWSLETTER LINK]
