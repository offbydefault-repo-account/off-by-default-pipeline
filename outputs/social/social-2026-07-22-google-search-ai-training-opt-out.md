### LinkedIn (~800 characters, max 1,800)

In June, Google sent an email to its users about a privacy change to Search Services. It looked like every other policy update: long, formal, legal header. Most people closed it.

Inside was something concrete: photos you send through Google Lens, voice recordings from voice queries, and files you upload to Google Translate would now be used to train Google's AI models. By default.

Before you close this: this is not about Google Photos. Your camera roll and backed-up pictures are explicitly excluded. What changed is the search input side — the things you used Google as a live tool to process.

And those inputs are often more sensitive than what's in your photo library. The rash you Lens'd to check on your kid's arm. The IRS letter you couldn't parse. The prescription label you photographed. You weren't trying to document those. You were trying to understand them.

The opt-out exists. It takes three minutes. Go to myactivity.google.com, find Search Services History, and turn off Save Media.
