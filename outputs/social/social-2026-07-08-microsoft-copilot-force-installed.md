## LINKEDIN

### Newsletter angle

**Variant 1:**

Earlier this year, Microsoft announced it would automatically install its AI assistant on Windows 11 computers during routine software updates. Users were unhappy. Coverage wasn't kind. Microsoft paused the rollout, citing a "technical issue." A lot of people moved on.

The rollout resumed in June. It's running through mid-July.

Copilot is Microsoft's AI sidebar — it connects to your Microsoft 365 account and can access your Outlook inbox, Word files, and OneDrive. It's similar to ChatGPT, except it runs inside your operating system with default access to your files.

The opt-out exists, but it was designed for organizations with IT administrators — teams that manage devices for large companies. Individual users, freelancers, and small businesses weren't given an equivalent control. The people with the least oversight got the least protection.

Users in the EU and EEA are completely exempt. EU law makes forcing software onto someone's device without consent illegal. Microsoft knows this, which is why they carved out that region while continuing everywhere else. That isn't a technicality — it's a signal about what kind of behavior requires a legal consequence to stop.

If you want to check what's on your machine: Start menu → Settings → Apps → Installed apps → search "Copilot." If it's there and you didn't put it there, you can uninstall it from that screen. This week's newsletter covers what Copilot can access, how to review your Microsoft data, and what the resumption of this rollout means. [NEWSLETTER LINK]
