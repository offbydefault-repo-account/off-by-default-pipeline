## LINKEDIN

> There's a setting in your Google account called "Search Services History" that probably didn't exist the last time you checked.
>
> In June 2026, Google created it to store photos, voice recordings, and files you upload through its search tools — Google Lens, AI Mode, Google Maps, Translate. By default, that data is used to train Google's AI models.
>
> Most people found out from an easy-to-miss email with a subject line about "Search Services."
>
> The data here is meaningfully different from search text. A photo of a medication bottle. A document you uploaded to ask an AI question. A voice search recorded while driving. These are richer, more contextual, more personal — and they were folded into the same opt-out framework as your regular search history, quietly, in June.
>
> The opt-out is real and not complicated: go to myactivity.google.com, sign in, and find the Search Services History tab. You can turn off everything, or just uncheck "Save Media" to stop photo and audio collection while keeping your text history. About 90 seconds.
>
> Worth knowing: turning it off doesn't delete what's already been collected. That's a separate step on the same page. And this setting is separate from your Web & App Activity setting — changing one doesn't affect the other.
>
> Full breakdown, with step-by-step instructions: [NEWSLETTER LINK]
