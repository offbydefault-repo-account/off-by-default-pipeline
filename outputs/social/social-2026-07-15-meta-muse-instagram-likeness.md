## LINKEDIN

> For 96 hours last week, any adult with a public Instagram account was automatically included as source material for a stranger's AI image generator.
>
> Meta launched Muse Image on July 8. Anyone could @-mention a public Instagram account in a prompt, and the AI would generate realistic images of that person — their face, their likeness, anyone visible in their photos, including children. No notification. No consent. Opt-out only, if you knew to look.
>
> SAG-AFTRA moved within 48 hours. Meta pulled the @-mention feature from Instagram on July 10.
>
> What didn't get pulled: Muse Image is still live on WhatsApp and the Meta AI app. Only the Instagram-specific source is gone. The technology and the business logic behind it remain.
>
> This is the fourth time in under two years that Meta has shipped a product with the same structure: assume consent, make it opt-out, absorb the backlash, adjust. The feature changes. The pattern doesn't.
>
> For anyone with a public account — for discoverability, client work, building an audience — "public" means something different now. Not because the rules changed, but because what companies can do with public photos keeps changing around us.
>
