## LINKEDIN

### Newsletter angle

**Variant 1:**

Google made a quiet announcement at its developer conference in May 2026 that's now taking effect.

The sparkle icon in Gmail — the one you could click if you wanted AI help, and ignore if you didn't — is being replaced by something different. Gemini isn't a button anymore. It's a layer underneath all of Google's products: Gmail, Docs, Drive, Sheets, and Search. Active by default. Not waiting for you to ask.

In Google Search, this is already visible: the AI-generated answer appears above the links. The people who wrote those pages are still not compensated when their work is summarized instead of visited. Traffic to original sources is measurably falling.

In Gmail and Docs, Gemini can read your content in real time to offer suggestions, summaries, and drafts. Google says it does not use your personal content to train future models — that's a meaningful distinction. But it's still access, and it's now on by default.

There are controls. They're in your Google account settings under Data & Privacy. They're not hard to find — they're just not advertised.

This week's newsletter covers what changed, what Gemini can access across your account, and what you can do about it. [NEWSLETTER LINK]
