## LINKEDIN (optimal ~800 characters)

In December 2025, Meta quietly updated its privacy policy to route conversations with Meta AI — on Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp — into its advertising targeting system. No announcement. No pop-up. No choice, if you're in the United States.

Here's what that looks like in practice: you ask Meta AI about hiking boots. That topic gets logged. You become someone with hiking-related interests. Ads for outdoor gear start appearing in your feed. Meta says sensitive topics — health, religion, politics — are excluded from this targeting. There's no audit trail you can check to verify that.

The piece of this most coverage skips: EU, UK, and South Korean users can opt out. Not because of different technology — Meta built the opt-out for those markets. American users weren't given it because regulators didn't require it.

This is a concrete example of something that's been abstract for years: your data rights in the US are not the same as your data rights in Europe. That difference now shows up in your ad feed.

If you use Meta AI for anything personal — health questions, financial questions, anything you'd say more quietly in conversation — it's worth understanding what it's become.
